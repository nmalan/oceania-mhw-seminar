About the Seminar

The Oceania Marine Heatwave Seminar brings together researchers from across Australia, New Zealand, New Caledonia, and international collaborators to share the latest findings in marine heatwave research.

Our monthly virtual seminars feature cutting-edge research on marine heatwave dynamics, impacts on marine ecosystems, prediction and modeling, and adaptation strategies.

Researchers from the US, China, and other international institutions regularly participate and present their work, fostering global collaboration on this critical topic.

Upcoming Talks

March 15, 2024 • 2:00 PM AEDT
Predictability of Marine Heatwaves in the Tasman Sea
Dr. Sarah Chen, University of Tasmania
This talk will examine subseasonal to seasonal predictability of marine heatwaves in the Tasman Sea using ensemble ocean forecasting systems. We explore the role of ocean preconditioning and atmospheric forcing in determining forecast skill.

Past Talks

February 9, 2024
Ecological Impacts of the 2015/16 Tasman Sea Marine Heatwave
Prof. Gretta Pecl, University of Tasmania
Examining the cascading effects of the 2015/16 marine heatwave on Tasman Sea ecosystems, from shifts in species distributions to impacts on commercial fisheries and coastal communities.

Marine Heatwave Dynamics in the Western Pacific Warm Pool
Dr. Ming Liu, Ocean University of China
An investigation of marine heatwave characteristics and drivers in the western Pacific warm pool region, with implications for coral reef systems and tropical cyclone intensification.
